Overview

The entry point to AMG.
Far from entry-level.

The 2026 Mercedes-AMG A35 distils decades of motorsport expertise into a compact hot hatch that you can genuinely use every day. It isn't the most extreme AMG — that's the point. It's the one you'll actually love living with.

⚡

Genuine AMG Engineering

The M260 2.0-litre turbocharged four-cylinder is hand-assembled at AMG's Affalterbach plant. Every engine bears the signature of the engineer who built it.

🏁

4MATIC+ All-Wheel Drive

The variable-torque 4MATIC+ system sends up to 50% of power to the rear axle on demand, giving the A35 genuinely playful rear-biased handling when you want it.

🎯

AMG Track Pace

Built-in lap timer and real-time telemetry via the MBUX infotainment system — because a car with this performance should help you use it properly.

🇬🇧

Built for British Roads

AMG Ride Control suspension has been specifically tuned for UK conditions. Firm enough to feel alive, compliant enough to handle British road surfaces.

Design

Form that follows
serious function.

The AMG A35 doesn't shout. It communicates with precision — wider arches, a Panamericana grille, quad exhausts, and a rear diffuser that all have aerodynamic purpose, not just aesthetic intent.

01

AMG Panamericana Grille

The signature vertical louvre grille — first seen on the AMG GT race cars — is unique to AMG models and provides superior airflow to the engine cooling system. Twelve vertical vanes, chrome-accented on Premium Plus.

02

Wider Bodywork

The A35 is 40mm wider than the standard A-Class, with flared front and rear arches housing a wider track. This isn't a visual trick — the wider stance directly improves cornering stability.

03

Quad Exhaust Tips

Four chromed exhaust outlets — a traditional AMG hallmark. The A35's active exhaust system delivers a characterful burble at startup and a proper bark when you push past 4,000 rpm.

04

AMG-Specific Rear Diffuser

The prominent rear diffuser, integrated rear spoiler, and bumper design generate meaningful downforce. Not just visual aggression — it keeps the rear planted at speed.

05

AMG 5-Twin-Spoke Alloys

Standard 18-inch AMG 5-twin-spoke alloys, with 19-inch forged items available as an option. The 19s noticeably sharpen steering response and reduce unsprung weight.

06

Night Package Option

The Night Package swaps chrome accents for gloss black — grille surrounds, mirror caps, boot lid spoiler. A popular UK choice that gives the A35 a more menacing, stealthy character.

Interior

A cockpit built around
the driver.

The AMG A35's interior is unmistakably sporty without being uncomfortable. The dual 10.25-inch screens, Nappa leather, and AMG-specific digital instruments create a driver-focused cabin that rewards daily use.

🪑

AMG Sport Seats

Standard sports seats with prominent bolstering hold you in place through corners without becoming tiring on longer journeys. The electrically adjustable AMG Performance seats (optional) add heating, memory, and ventilation.

📱

MBUX Dual-Screen Setup

Two 10.25-inch displays merge seamlessly. The driver's screen replaces traditional instruments entirely; the central display handles navigation, media, and climate. Touch, voice, and touch-pad all work.

🏎

AMG Steering Wheel

The flat-bottomed, Nappa-leather-trimmed AMG Performance steering wheel carries drive mode paddles and individual controls for driving dynamics settings — change modes without taking your eyes off the road.

💡

64-Colour Ambient Lighting

The interior lighting system offers 64 colours and intensity levels — and syncs with the active drive mode. In Race mode it shifts to red; Comfort keeps things to softer blues and whites. A small detail that genuinely adds to the experience.

🔊

Burmester Surround Sound

The optional Burmester® surround sound system features 13 speakers with 590W of output — one of the best factory audio installations available in a compact car at any price point.

📊

AMG Track Pace Display

The MBUX shows real-time G-force, lap time, engine data, tyre temperature (if sensors fitted), and accelerometer graphs directly on the centre screen. The data-logger function records entire sessions for later analysis.

Technology

Intelligent by default.
Exceptional when needed.

Active Brake Assist

Pre-emptively primes the brakes and applies emergency braking if a collision is imminent — even with cross-traffic. Standard on all A35 variants.

Active Lane Keeping Assist

Monitors lane markings and applies gentle corrective steering if you drift. Works alongside active speed limit assist for motorway driving.

PRE-SAFE® System

If a collision is unavoidable, PRE-SAFE tightens seatbelts, closes windows, and adjusts seat position in milliseconds — before the impact.

Adaptive Highbeam

Intelligent headlights that adjust beam pattern to avoid dazzling other drivers while maintaining maximum forward visibility for you. Standard from Premium Plus.

Blind Spot Monitoring

Radar sensors monitor your blind spots and warn you before you change lanes. Active lane change assist guides you back if you signal toward an occupied space.

Drowsiness Detection

Attention Assist monitors your steering behaviour patterns over time and warns you if it detects signs of fatigue — particularly useful on long motorway journeys.

Hey Mercedes Voice Control

Natural language voice control for navigation, media, phone calls, and climate. Works offline for core functions — no network dependency for most tasks.

Live Traffic Integration

Real-time traffic data overlaid on the navigation map, with automatic route recalculation. Updates every 2 minutes via the Mercedes me connect service.

Mercedes me App

Remote lock/unlock, pre-conditioning the cabin temperature, checking fuel level and tyre pressures, and locating the car in a car park — all from your phone.

Augmented Reality Navigation

Available with the Premium Plus package — the navigation overlays directional arrows onto a live camera feed of the road ahead, displayed on the central screen.

Over-the-Air Updates

Mercedes pushes software updates wirelessly overnight, including navigation maps, MBUX feature improvements, and driving dynamics refinements.

Digital Key

Share a digital car key with up to 5 people via the Mercedes me app. Enables keyless access and starting using NFC-enabled smartphones.

AMG Track Pace

Lap timer, sector timing, real-time G-force display, accelerometer graphs, and a full data logger. Records your entire session and lets you review it afterwards.

AMG Dynamic Plus Package

Optional package adding Race mode with fully variable torque split and a drift mode (up to 100% rear torque available) — essentially turns the A35 into a different car.

AMG Ride Control Suspension

Electronically adjustable dampers change stiffness continuously — 1,000 adjustments per second per damper. Different maps per drive mode.

AMG Torque Control

The ESP system can selectively brake individual wheels in cornering to sharpen turn-in. Rear-biased during Sport+ and Race for a naturally agile feel.

Sport Exhaust System

Active exhaust flap system opens under hard acceleration, producing a raspy, characterful exhaust note without resonance at motorway cruise speeds.

AMG Performance Display

Dedicated digital instrument display showing engine output %, torque distribution, and tyre temperature — all on the driver's main screen.

Wireless Apple CarPlay

Full wireless Apple CarPlay — no cable needed. Connects automatically when your iPhone is nearby. Standard on all 2026 A35 models.

Wireless Android Auto

Wireless Android Auto is standard. Works alongside CarPlay — you can switch between smartphone mirrors and native MBUX any time.

Wireless Charging Pad

15W wireless phone charging pad in the centre console, compatible with Qi-standard devices. Can charge while also using the wireless CarPlay/Auto connection.

LTE Hotspot

Built-in 4G LTE WiFi hotspot supports up to 8 devices simultaneously. Connected to Mercedes' high-speed data network — not dependent on your phone signal.

USB-C Ports

Multiple USB-C ports, including two in the rear for passengers. The front ports support fast charging and data transfer for CarPlay/Auto wired fallback.

Spotify In-Car

Native Spotify integration in MBUX — browse and control your Spotify account directly from the main screen without the need for phone mirroring.

Ownership

The real cost of living
with an A35.

The sticker price is only the beginning. Here's what the first three years of A35 ownership actually looks like in the UK.

Running Costs Summary

⛽

Fuel

Real-world 28–32 mpg on a mix of commuting, B-roads, and motorway. Expect £250–£350/month for a typical 12,000-mile UK driver. Always use 98 RON (e.g., Shell V-Power) — the ECU advances timing with higher octane fuel and performance improves measurably.

🔧

Servicing

Mercedes-Benz Service Care package recommended. Annual A-service (minor): ~£350; annual B-service (major): ~£550. Service intervals are variable — typically 15,500 miles or annually. Independent Mercedes-specialist garages are a valid and cost-effective option from year 3 onwards.

🛡️

Insurance

The A35 sits in insurance groups 42–44 (out of 50). A 30-year-old with a clean licence and 3+ years' no-claims in a postcode like M4 can expect to pay £1,400–£1,900 annually. Shop annually via broker — MercedesBenz-specific insurers (e.g., Adrian Flux) can offer better rates.

🚗

VED (Road Tax)

First-year VED: £220 (included in OTR price). Subsequent years: £195/year standard rate. No additional premium for vehicles over £40,000 — the "expensive car supplement" (£620/year) does not apply as it only affects vehicles above £40,000 registered from April 2025, but the A35 exceeds this. Check with your dealer on current rules.

🏎

Tyres

Front tyres on an A35 last approximately 15,000–20,000 miles in normal use. Rears last slightly longer. Budget ~£180–£220 per tyre for quality replacements (Michelin Pilot Sport 5, Continental SportContact 7). Do not cheap out on tyres on this car.

📉

Depreciation

Mercedes hot hatches typically depreciate 40–50% in the first three years. A35 bought new at £43,500 may be worth ~£23,000–£26,000 at 3 years/36,000 miles. Desirable colours (Cosmos Black, Cavansite Blue) and optional packs improve resale. Keep full dealer service history.

New Owner Checklist

☐

Register your Mercedes me account

Set up the Mercedes me app before you drive away. Activate connected services — you get 3 years of premium connectivity included.

☐

Explore all 5 drive modes

Take 30 minutes on a quiet road to experience all modes. Race mode especially — you need to feel the difference before you're in a situation where it matters.

☐

Set up AMG Track Pace

Even if you never go to a circuit, calibrating AMG Track Pace shows you how to use the MBUX performance displays during spirited road driving.

☐

Switch to 98 RON fuel

Use Shell V-Power or BP Ultimate from day one. The ECU uses knock sensors to advance timing with higher octane — genuine power and economy benefit.

☐

Book into an AMG Driving Experience

Mercedes-AMG UK runs half- and full-day driving experiences at Thruxton, Bedford Autodrome, and Snetterton. Excellent way to safely explore the car's capabilities with AMG instructors.

☐

Consider GAP insurance

Given the A35's depreciation curve, GAP insurance (typically £150–£300 for 3 years) protects you if the car is written off and your insurer pays out less than the finance settlement.

☐

Check tyre pressures weekly for the first month

New cars can show slight pressure loss as tyres bed in. The A35 runs slightly higher tyre pressures than most cars — check the sticker inside the driver's door jamb.

☐

Configure your personal Drive Mode preset

The MBUX allows you to save a custom drive mode config — e.g., Sport+ damping with Comfort throttle map. Build your daily-use sweet spot.

Long-Term Ownership Tips

01

Run-in Period

Avoid sustained high rpm for the first 1,000 miles. Vary speeds and don't thrash it from cold. The engine will reward a careful run-in with better longevity and potentially better peak performance.

02

Warm Up Before Sport+/Race

The DCT and AWD system need oil temperature before they perform optimally. In cold UK weather, drive gently for 5–10 minutes before selecting Sport+ or Race. This isn't just good practice — it protects the gearbox.

03

Service at a Mercedes Specialist

From year 3–4, consider AMG-trained independent specialists. They use genuine parts and AMG diagnostic tools but cost 25–40% less than franchised dealers. The Mercedes-Benz Independent Technicians (MBISOC) directory is a good starting point.

04

Keep the Dealer Service History

A full Mercedes-Benz dealer service history adds £2,000–£3,500 to the resale value versus an independent-only history. Weigh the cost savings against the resale premium before switching.

05

Winter Tyres Are Worth It

The 4MATIC+ AWD is genuinely impressive in the wet but all-season performance on cheap all-season tyres is poor. A dedicated set of winter tyres (Michelin CrossClimate 2 or Continental WinterContact TS 870) on steel rims transforms the car from November to March.

06

Join the Community

The Mercedes-AMG Owners Club (MAOC) and the A35 threads on MBClub UK and PistonHeads are invaluable. Real-world fault reports, spec advice, and recommended service centres — worth £80/year membership for the knowledge alone.

The Honest Verdict

If you want the fastest car in this segment and budget isn't a constraint, the Audi RS3 is the benchmark. If pure driver engagement matters above all else, the Honda Civic Type R is technically more rewarding and significantly cheaper. And if you want the full AMG flagship experience in a compact, the A45 S delivers with its 421 hp and 3.9-second sprint — at a £13,500 premium.

The AMG A35 wins on the combination of AMG brand prestige, interior quality, 4MATIC+ versatility, and a genuinely usable everyday character. It's not the fastest, not the cheapest, not the most practical. But as a complete package for someone who wants a daily driver that's also genuinely exciting — it's very difficult to beat at the price.

Why we included the Kawasaki Ninja ZX-10R

The ZX-10R is a 210 hp litre-class superbike built around a Superstock-derived engine. It will sprint to 62 mph in around 2.7 seconds — nearly two seconds quicker than the A35 — on roughly the same fuel and for less than half the purchase price. By those numbers alone it embarrasses a lot of expensive machinery.

But the A35 will carry your family, your luggage, a set of golf clubs, and get you there dry. It'll start on a cold Tuesday morning without drama, take a full motorway lane with 30 mm of standing water, and park in a multi-storey. The ZX-10R is extraordinary at one thing: going very fast in ideal conditions. The A35 is genuinely excellent at everything else. They are not competitors. The ZX-10R is here as a reminder that fast is relative — and that what you actually need matters more than any single number on a spec sheet.

The A35 exists to be fast, fun, and comfortable in the real world most UK drivers inhabit. Whether you're buying new from a Mercedes-AMG Centre or hunting for a used example, here's how to approach the process without leaving money on the table.

New vs. Used

Buying New

  • Full 3-year/unlimited mileage warranty
  • 2026 spec (latest MBUX, features)
  • Your exact colour and options
  • New car finance deals from Mercedes-Benz Financial
  • First service included in many packages
  • Complimentary breakdown cover (3 years)

Downside: you take the biggest depreciation hit in year 1 (~15–20% of value).

Buying Used

  • Avoid the steepest depreciation
  • 2–3 year old A35s at ~£27,000–£33,000
  • Approved Used gives 1-year warranty
  • Can often get Premium Plus at a price of base new
  • Real-world reliability data available
  • Lower PCP payments for same spec

Downside: history check essential; warranty costs after 3 years can be significant.

Dealer Negotiation Tips

1

Time it right

Mercedes-AMG dealers have quarterly targets. End of March, June, September, and December are the best times to negotiate — dealers are more motivated to close deals.

2

Ask for service packages

You're unlikely to get significant money off the OTR price. Instead, negotiate free accessories (mats, first service, extended warranty, or paint protection film) — these have higher margin to play with.

3

Get multiple quotes

Use the Mercedes-Benz online configurator to spec your car, then contact 3–4 AMG Centres. The market is national — you don't have to buy locally. Transport can be arranged.

4

Understand the finance

Dealer PCP can be competitive, but compare against specialist car finance brokers (Zopa, Evolution Funding, Monzo Flex). Sometimes a personal loan beats PCP on total cost.

5

Check stock models

Dealers often have stock units with options already added at a discount to their individual option prices. A stock car in a colour you like with Night Package and metallic paint already fitted can represent strong value.

Used A35 — What to Check

✓

Full dealer service history

Critical. Confirm stamps are from Mercedes-Benz franchised dealers or AMG-approved specialists. Gaps in history are a red flag.

!

DCT gearbox behaviour

The 7G DCT should be completely smooth at walking-pace manoeuvring. Judder, clunk, or hesitation suggests clutch wear — a costly fix (£3,000–£5,000). Test in a car park at low speed.

!

Track day use

Ask directly. Check the car's history for track day entries. Hard track use stresses brakes, tyres, and cooling. Not necessarily a deal-breaker, but factor it in.

✓

HPI / MOT check

Run a full HPI check (£15–£20 via autotrader or hpicheck.com). Check MOT history at check.mot.gov.uk — advisories tell you a lot about how the car has been maintained.

!

Tyre condition and age

Check the date code on each tyre (e.g., 1821 = week 18, 2021). Tyres over 6 years old should be replaced regardless of tread depth. Also check for uneven wear — indicates alignment or suspension issues.

✓

4MATIC+ all-wheel drive test

In a safe, empty car park, do a slow tight circle in both directions. Any binding, clicking, or vibration from the driveline needs investigation before purchase.
